An adventure. In the last two weeks, I (a retiree and senior citizen) spent all day, 7-to-7, 5D a week, learning how to use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build webpages and websites - but just the face and not the heart of it - at least, not yet! This website is the result of my efforts to learn.

Overall, I did enjoy the class but I also have to say that my experience or shall I say our experiences (as I noticed some of us did have a rather blank expression) could have been much better. Firstly, the organization of class could have been better-managed; by this I mean, norvices, and probably not so tech-savvy retirees like myself should have been separated from experienced or those already have superior knowledge and skills on using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. In that way, tutors may give extra attention to the weaker group. Our dedicated tutors could have been better-prepared or better-organised with specific steps of learning or templates, which individuals could follow by perhaps repeating them on a step-by-step basis. Secondly, the teaching and learning process could have been better. My understanding (and a few others) of a bootcamp course is a kind of hands-on theory-to-practice or concepts-to-practice learning process. Unfortunately, what I experienced was mostly self-learning. I spent most of my time watching and listening to videos on Next Academy portal, which is not always easy to follow and understand. Besides, there isn't enough time for me to watch all the videos in that short period of time. A better alternative would have been to have a better teaching-learning process. There should have been more specific supervised daily activities, for example, In a day, individuals could perform several steps of coding as targetd minimum learning outcomes for the day. After a few days or when they feel more confident i.e., when individuals have acquired the necessary skill, tutors can let them loose. In that way, some of us could, at least go home with some skills. I know that tutors encourage participants to ask, but to be honest, we don't quite know what to ask, especially in the first few days. Finally, the infrastructure could have been better too. Apart from a very high ratio of participants-to-tutors (20:2), the room is crowded and the temperature is not well-controlled. Some spots are freezing while others are warm. It might also be helpful, if lecturers use a clip-on or hands-free microphone so that the microphone is always close to his mouth and his hands can be freed to perform other tasks such as writing or typing. As it was, the audio quality was poor, in part from poor handling of microphone, and the rapid changes/switching on the projector screen gave us a headache - we cant quite follow on what was going on on the screen. In conclusion, a few others and I don't think that $$$ per day is a good value-for-money proposition. In fact, I just came to know of a participant who decided to break-off and left earlier.

Why did I join Next Academy bootcamp?


I'm a generation 'X' person. During my university days, I used a mannual typewriter to prepare my assignments. My first experience with a computer was with Amstrad (in the UK) in the 70s and then the Apple Macintosh. I only started using the Internet in the mid-1990s, i.e., on my return to the UK. Since then, the Internet has enjoyed massive adoption and is instrumental in helping to make organizations more prominent especially in the Internet world. Businesses which ignored the potential of the Internet did so at their own perils. In the past I was booged down with work and other responsibilities. I must now admit that I do not have any more excuses of not having the time to learn more about the cyberworld because the world is relying more on web-driven activities and the use of artificial intelligence (AI). Bricks and mortars premises will become less important and offices will be everywhere (mobile offices). Large sucessful businesses and hence customers want more tasks and processes to be done over the Internet than through traditional physical face-to-face interactions. With that in mind, I am curious to know how various business activities could be enhanced by websites, for example, on how people interactions could be managed more productively.

Besides, I feel bored a week after leaving my part-time job (did that for three years after I left my full-time job). My brain is getting more inquisitive and I feel the need to learn something that is not only useful but also interesting. A few years a go, I had a business concept that need the use of a what I would then called a 'dynamic website' rather than a 'passive wesite', which were quite common and mainly as an information portal. I could not build one and so I asked a company to do it for me. They came back with a ridiculous quotation which a friend of mine advised me against accepting it. I put my ideas on hold while I search for a solution. As the years went by the world becomes more internet-dependent and everyone can't leave home without his/her smart phone. Hmm...I thought I need to do something. Anyway, after two weeks in this bootcamp, I found that that quotation was a probably a 'cheat!' By the way, I came to know about Next Academy from my daughter (a millennial) who, understandably, is more attuned with the Internet world.

In summary, I joined this bootcamp, primarly, to acquire the skills of building a working websites that would be useful and can be monetised.

Had I benefited from this boot camp?


Would you like to know my rating on benefits gained?

Yes, I did benefit mainly in terms of knowing where to find potential sources of information on the Internet (via uncle 'Google'), from which I may be able to learn the 'how' to use HTML and CSS meaningfully. Har har har...in hindsight, these are things, which I could have explored on my own. The most useful experience, I would say, is probably having had the opportunity to meet new friends. To me, our tutors became more useful when we were learning JavaScript, which can be a bit tricky for an 'old engine' like myself to follow the logic and to recognize or remember the various syntax, tags, and anatomy of them. But I must say after exploring and doing and repetedly doing, one begins to get-the-hang of learning HTML, CSS, and JS. It's about getting to one of those destinations via many paths.

So, on the second last day of this two-week course/programme, I decided against continuing with the back-end part of programme - that would have been another eight weeks of ....... At my current level of skills, I feel that that I will not benefit much and may even make me feel stressful.
